About

I studied Computer Science at Bennett University, where I got hooked on understanding how systems work beneath the surface, from distributed architectures to the infrastructure that holds everything together at scale.

I spent four years at Addepar building a trading and rebalancing platform from the ground up. 12+ microservices, DAG-based compute architecture, and deep performance work across the entire stack. That experience taught me that the hardest part of engineering isn't writing code. It's designing systems that stay maintainable as they grow.

Now at Paytm, I'm working on merchant onboarding systems that handle 500+ TPS and support 20M+ merchants.

I approach problems by breaking them down to first principles. I'd rather build something simple that scales reliably than something clever that breaks under pressure. Long-term, I'm focused on distributed systems, platform engineering, and building infrastructure that teams can depend on.

Work

Systems I've built professionally. Real scale, real constraints, real impact.

Merchant Onboarding Platform, Paytm

Paytm needed highly available, read-optimized microservices to support merchant onboarding at massive scale. 20M+ merchants, 500+ TPS.

Built a DB-driven dynamic preference framework that made onboarding flows configurable without code changes, reducing overall development effort by ~70%. Enabled customization of DCC markup values and contributed to marquee merchant acquisition.

JavaSpring BootMicroservicesPostgreSQLKafka

At this scale, configurability beats custom code. A well-designed framework that lets the business move without engineering bottlenecks has outsized impact. ~$6M/year GMV in this case.

Trading & Rebalancing System, Addepar

Advisors and traders needed an automated system to generate trades for portfolios based on models, restrictions, equivalents, and tax optimizations, with sub-300ms processing time per input.

Built 12+ microservices from initial development through live deployment. Designed a compute DAG architecture to decompose complex workflows into reusable components. Reduced rebalancing workflow time by 40% by converting a microservice into a shared library. Secured PII across 3+ schemas using Pgcrypto.

JavaMicroservicesDAG ArchitecturePostgreSQLPgcryptoSumologic

DAG-based architecture is powerful for decomposing complex financial workflows. Independent monitoring of compute components made debugging production issues dramatically faster. TDD with 80%+ coverage kept the system stable through years of iteration.

Nutrition Store & Gym Membership Platform

A health & fitness company needed a unified platform integrating supplement sales, gym memberships, and analytics, with loyalty features to drive retention.

Developed the entire Django application end-to-end: backend APIs, admin panel, customer flows. Designed a "FitPoints" loyalty system that increased retention and repeat purchases by 60%. Managed full AWS deployment (EC2, RDS, S3, SES).

PythonDjangoAWSEC2RDSS3SES

End-to-end ownership forces you to think about the full picture, from database schema to email deliverability. The loyalty system taught me that small product features can have outsized business impact.

Portfolio Management System

Investors needed a system to manage and analyze portfolios across time segments with key financial metrics (EBITDA, Equity, Cash Flow, Profit, Margin Assets).

Developed core services including entitlements and authentication. Integrated Okta for seamless SSO with client accounts. Implemented JWT-based auth via Spring Security and designed the password recovery workflow.

JavaSpring BootSpring SecurityOktaJWT

Authentication and entitlements are deceptively complex in multi-tenant financial systems. Getting the security layer right early saves enormous pain later.
